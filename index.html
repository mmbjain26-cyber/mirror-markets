Pairing 001 Stripe|UPI & Pix

The take rate goes to zero. Then what?

In the US, moving money is a business. Stripe, Adyen and the card networks earn a slice of every transaction, and that slice is the product. In India and Brazil, the state built the rails and priced them at zero. UPI and Pix now move more volume than most card networks, and nobody earns a take rate on the transfer itself.

The interesting question is not which system wins. It is what happens to a payments business model when the thing it charges for becomes free. Monetization moves up the stack, into credit, float, and software. One market is already living in that future. The other is priced as if it never arrives.
